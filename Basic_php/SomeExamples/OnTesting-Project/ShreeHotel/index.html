History about DIGHA

In India, we respect our visitors as God and chant the line ´atithi devo bhava´ very often. Even our almighty has given numbers of places to quench nature lovers´ thirst and our west Bengal is one of them. In west Bengal, Digha is one of those places, where nature calls its visitors again and again.
Digha has mainly risen on the place once known as Beerkul during British Ancient. Digha's official recorded history in the recent past started in 1770s when India's first Governor-General - Warren Hastings mentioned Digha as 'Brighton of the East' in his letters to his wife - Mary and other British officials.
It was in 1923 when John Frank Smith - an English tourist made Digha his home and began writing about this place. His writings also persuaded Dr. Bidhan Chandra Roy - West Bengal Chief Minister to develop Digha as a beach resort.
Since the time that Digha's presence was officially recorded, the town was in the hands of the British East India Company right up till the time of Indian Independence in 1947.
The 7-kilometre long Golden-sandy Beach laced with cashew plantations on its sand dunes and shallow swimmable sea of 1 kilometre depth is what put Digha on the map. It is possible today to reach Digha by Helicopter service. Thus, no doubt, it is developing to grab attention of visitors.
