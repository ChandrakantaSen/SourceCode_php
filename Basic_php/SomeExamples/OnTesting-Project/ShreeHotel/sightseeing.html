Places of Interest




BEACH FESTIVAL

Digha celebrates beach festival every year, in the month of February. Digha will amaze you with mostly admired sea food festival in West Bengal, where you can choose your favorite fish and get it cooked. Amazing dishes, made of pomfret, prawn, Betki, Hilsa etc. give Digha another identity.

Please Note : Beside accommodation and fooding we will arrange Digha Safari (as per your preference) to entertain you with all these below mentioned facilities.

NEW DIGHA BEACH

New Digha is 2 km from the old beach. New Digha is much wider than old Digha. Moreover, the stretch from old Digha to new Digha is totally filled with Casuarina plant that gives utmost pleasure to view while travelling. Different spices of birds, calm waves along with beautiful sunrise and sunset have given Digha a charm to let its visitors spellbound. New Digha beach is covered with hard sands, same as old Digha beach, on which one can drive during low tide.

MARINE AQUARIUM AND RESEARCH CENTRE

It has been established in the year of 1989 to make visitors aware about the marine biodiversity of the region. This place lures the enthusiastic mind for further researches to take place. This marine Aquarium and research center lies between the old Digha and new Digha. This aquarium is well equipped with sea water circulation system and modern filtration. It represents itself as a largest aquarium in coastal India.

CHANDANESHWAR (A TEMPLE OF LORD SHIVA)

This place is 8 km far from Digha and known for the temple of lord Shiva. It is near Bengal and Orissa border. A huge annual fair Pana Sankranti is celebrated here on the first day of Odiya calendar, every year. Even a popular annual fair “Chadak Mela” takes place during the month of Chaitra (March 15 to April 13), as per the Bengali calendar. More than half million people gather in Chadak Mela every year. Hence, it is called second Puri of Orissa. This Chadak Mela continues for 13 days. To accept the paita (pabitrata) devotees come here every year in this time. The devotees do their worship for 4 days and during these days they fast whole day and offer arghya (prayer) to the lord Shiva. During this festival devotees stab themselves with iron rod or thorny stem.

BICHITROPUR

One who loves to feel the nature closely, he or she should at least visit the place Bichitrapur in Balasore. It will take approximately 2 hrs from Digha. Different species of birds and mangrove forest make scenes more enchanting to the visitors. Gathering of red crabs and mangrove forest reminds visitors about Sundarban Forest, near Bay of Bengal. Near bichitropur, there is another temple named vusandeswar mandir and very few know about one of the largest Shiva Lingas is worshiped here. People say about mythological history of treta yug, when Ravana went to bring lord Shiva with him to Lnaka. Ravana was on his way but failed to carry the Shiv Linga due to the conspiracy of other gods and angels and placed lord Shiva here. He tried to lift Shiva Linga a lot but failed due to the heaviness of it. Since then, god Shiva is worshiped here.

TALSARI

Talsari is the centre of attraction due to its close acquaintance with Subarnarekha River and Sea. It is 6 KM away from Digha and placed at Orissa-Bengal border with a small shape of Delta. The reason behind such name is the series of palm trees that have surrounded the place, i.e., Tala (palm) sari (row). During the full moon the beach becomes a dreamland. Even the word Tala also means rhythm, which you can get to hear during the lapping of waves against the sea shore. As it is a hard beach, it makes walk smoother. During high tides you can reach the main beach with the help of a boat, otherwise you may take long walk towards the main beach during low tides. The most attracting feature is the backwater that you need to cross to reach the seaside. Even you can see the Subarnarekha River from the distance.

UDAIPUR BEACH

Udaipur enchants its visitors with its virgin nature, i.e., till now it is less civilized than other beaches. It is approximately 2 KM west from Digha. The unique nature of this beach is the half of the hotel lies in West Bengal and another half in Orissa. Moreover, tourists can hire bikes to have ride along the beach in expense of 30 per hour. Even red crabs and tortoise are the main attraction to the tourists.

SCIENCE CENTRE

One of the main attraction of Digha is Science centre, which is established by national Council of Science Museums. This visiting place is quite attractive to the children. People who come with their families for vacation should visit science centre. The planetarium is there that almost resembles as Eskimo’s Igloo, Jurassic age park and fun glasses to measure height, 3D film show, sky observation, science magic show are the main attraction here.

KANTHI

Previously, it was an abundant port and in later period people started calling it as Kendua, which has later been changed to Contain and last of all this beach was named as Kanthi. Though, people tell about a local history, where sadhus and fakirs try to cure people and ask them about “kahan thee”, that means “where are you from”. In later period the cure seeking people started identifying the location, where people utters “khana thee” and that was merged later to call it as kanthi. It is 33 KM away from Digha.

SUBARNAREKHA RIVER

Most of the visitors don’t know about reason behind such name. Few decades ago gold was mined at the source of this river, i.e., in Ranchi. After this, people started calling it Subarnarekha, “streak of gold”. Subarnarekha River is flowing towards Bay of Bengal with its smooth flow and attracts its visitors Due to its calmness. It is 12 KM away from Digha. It is one of the calm and peaceful places, where you can enjoy your solitude or a mindful conversation with your loved ones.

MANDARMANI BEACH

This location is mostly admired for picnic spot, which is 20 KM away from Chaulkhola on the way of Digha. With calm waves and cleanliness, it serves a relaxing atmosphere to the visitors. It is 13 km long beach. Moreover, beach biking, para gliding is available here. Even few local people arrange a short trip of Mohona at the time of sunset. Though, it has comparatively low waves than Digha. Very few people know that Mandarmani was previously known as Mandarmoni.

AMRABATI PARK

With the Boating facilities and eye pleasing beauty of nature, it attracts a huge numbers of visitors every year. It takes 5 minutes from the New Digha beach. This park enchants its visitors with its beauty and calm nature.

TOY TRAIN

Children and fun loving people will enjoy the toy train ride at the kajal Dighi Park. It is 1.5 far from digha. You have heard about it or experienced a ride of toy train in Darjeeling that let your mind crawl to discover the nature being present in its lap. Even Nicco Park in Kolkata also provides toy train ride inside the park. Toy train reminds you hilly areas, bushes, trees that try to hug the train with its bent stem and the train keeps moving, leaving all the tangles behind. Kajal Dighi Park at Digha have arranged it, where visitors take the pleasure of toy train ride around the flourish Kajal dighi park where tangle of bushes, eye pleasing flowery garden, small pool let the visitors feel the charm of nature and toy train too. .

JUNPUT

Junput is 40 km away from Digha, by bus. You can reach there by rickshaw from kanthi easily. Sunrise in Junput attracts numbers of visitors due to its eye pleasing view. Those who have come to visit Digha, must spent some time at Junput. The calm waves and breeze will amaze you taking all your tiredness within few seconds. Close association of Light House and Kopalkundola temple will make your visit at Junput much worthy. .

While coming back from Junput, get down at Petua on the way of Rasulpur and with a 3-4 KM walk, you can easily reach Dariapur, where a famous old temple Kapalkundala is there. This the place, where Bankim chandra chattopadhay got inspiration to write his second novel Kopalkundola.
